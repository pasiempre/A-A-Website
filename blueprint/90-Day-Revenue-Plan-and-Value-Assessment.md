## 6) KPI Framework and Thresholds

- **Green** = on-track
- **Yellow** = needs optimization
- **Red** = intervention required

#### Lead Response Time (business hours)
- Green: <= 15 min
- Yellow: 16-30 min
- Red: > 30 min

#### Lead-to-Quote Rate
- Green: >= 55%
- Yellow: 40%-54%
- Red: < 40%

#### Quote-to-Close Rate
- Green: >= 30%
- Yellow: 20%-29%
- Red: < 20%

#### Cost per Lead (blended paid)
- Green: Within target band set at launch
- Yellow: 10%-25% above target
- Red: > 25% above target

- Green: <= 5%
- Yellow: 6%-10%
- Red: > 10%

#### Review Velocity (Google)
- Green: >= 2 new quality reviews/month
- Yellow: 1/month

## 8) Budget Tiers (Ads Only)

- Lean: **$1.5k-$3k/month**
- Growth: **$3k-$7k/month**
- Aggressive local scale: **$7k-$15k+/month**

Creative/content production is separate and should be budgeted independently.

### 8.1 When to Change Budget Tiers

Move from Lean → Growth when ALL are true:
- [ ] CPL is within target band for 2+ consecutive weeks
- [ ] Lead-to-Quote rate is ≥ 45%
- [ ] Lead response SLA is consistently green (≤ 15 min)
- [ ] Crew capacity has ≥ 25% available slots for next 2 weeks

Move from Growth → Aggressive when ALL are true:
- [ ] Quote-to-Close rate is ≥ 28%
- [ ] ROAS is ≥ 2.5x for 3+ consecutive weeks
- [ ] Crew capacity can absorb 30%+ more volume (or hiring pipeline is active)
- [ ] Quality complaints are not trending upward

Scale back when ANY are true:
- [ ] Crew capacity is ≥ 90% booked with no hiring pipeline
- [ ] CPL exceeds target by > 25% for 2 consecutive weeks
- [ ] Lead response SLA is consistently red (> 30 min)
- [ ] Quality complaints are increasing month-over-month

Platform assist: F-30 (Capacity Throttle Signal) automates capacity warnings; until shipped, check dispatch capacity manually every Monday.

## 9) Best-Version Vision

The best version of this system is a closed-loop operating model:

1. High-intent traffic lands on trust-matched pages.
2. Leads are captured and contacted quickly with consistent scripts.
3. Quotes move fast with clear proof and follow-up cadence.
4. Ops and field execution are visible and reliable.
5. Completion proof feeds social proof and paid creative.
6. Weekly optimization reallocates spend to highest-return segments.

That loop compounds conversion efficiency over time.

## 14) Revenue Forecasting Model

Purpose: Turn abstract KPIs into concrete "if we spend X, we should see Y" projections that mom can understand and hold the team accountable to.

### 14.1 Unit Economics Formula

```
Monthly Revenue = Leads × Lead-to-Quote Rate × Quote-to-Close Rate × Avg Job Value

Example (Lean Budget):
40 leads × 50% quoted × 28% closed = 5.6 jobs
5.6 jobs × $850 avg value = $4,760/month revenue

Example (Growth Budget):
90 leads × 55% quoted × 30% closed = 14.9 jobs
14.9 jobs × $900 avg value = $13,410/month revenue
```

## 15) Customer Lifetime Value Model

Purpose: Recurring contracts are the economic engine of a cleaning business. This section models why converting one-time jobs into recurring accounts is the highest-leverage growth move.

### 15.1 One-Time vs Recurring Value

| Customer Type | Avg Job Value | Frequency | Annual Value | Acquisition Cost | Payback |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| One-time | $900 | 1x | $900 | $150-$300 | Immediate |
| Recurring (weekly) | $350/visit | 50x/year | $17,500 | $150-$300 | Week 1 |
| Recurring (biweekly) | $450/visit | 26x/year | $11,700 | $150-$300 | Visit 1 |
| Recurring (monthly) | $600/visit | 12x/year | $7,200 | $150-$300 | Visit 1 |

### 15.2 The Compounding Math

```
1 new recurring weekly client per month for 6 months:

Month 1:  1 client  × $350/wk = $1,400/mo
Month 2:  2 clients × $350/wk = $2,800/mo
Month 3:  3 clients × $350/wk = $4,200/mo
Month 6:  6 clients × $350/wk = $8,400/mo

Annualized from Month 6 onward: $100,800/year
Total acquisition cost: ~$1,800
```

This is why every section of the platform — from quote templates to follow-up sequences to review generation — should subtly orient toward recurring conversion.

## 17) Sales Conversation Playbook

Purpose: The website gets the lead in the door. This section covers what happens in the human conversation that turns the lead into a paying customer. Without this, conversion breaks at the human handoff.

### 17.1 Speed-to-Contact Scripts

> Platform assist: once P0 is live, customer auto-acknowledgment (F-02) should have already fired before callback.

**Phone callback (warm lead, < 15 min after form submission):**
> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from A&A Cleaning — you just submitted a request on our website and I wanted to reach out right away. Is now a good time to talk about what you need?"

**If voicemail:**
> "Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from A&A Cleaning. I saw your request come in and wanted to get back to you quickly. I'll send you a text right now with my direct number. Looking forward to helping you out."

**Follow-up text (immediately after call attempt):**
> "Hi [Name]! This is [Your Name] from A&A Cleaning. I just tried calling about your cleaning request. When's a good time to chat? I can also answer questions here by text. 😊"

### 17.2 Discovery Questions (What to Ask)

The goal is not to pitch — it's to understand the job and qualify the lead.

| Question | Why You're Asking |
|---|---|
| "Tell me about the space — what kind of building/property?" | Service type + pricing tier |
| "What's the situation — is this a one-time need or ongoing?" | One-time vs recurring (huge LTV difference) |
| "When do you need it done by?" | Urgency = willingness to pay |
| "Is there anything specific you're concerned about?" | Objection surfacing early |
| "Have you worked with a cleaning company before?" | Competitive context + pain points |
| "What didn't work with the last company?" | Positioning opportunity |
| "Who else is involved in making this decision?" | Decision-maker mapping |
| "What's your budget range, roughly?" | Qualification + template selection |

### 17.3 Objection Responses

| Objection | Response Framework |
|---|---|
| "Your price is too high" | "I understand — can I ask what you're comparing against? Our pricing includes [insurance/supplies/quality guarantee/bilingual crews]. Most of our repeat clients tell us the consistency saves them money on recleans and complaints." |
| "I need to think about it" | "Of course. What's the main thing you'd want to be sure about? I can send you some before/after examples from similar jobs while you're deciding." |
| "I'm getting other quotes" | "That's smart — you should. What I'd say is: ask them about insurance, who's actually doing the work, and what happens if you're not happy. We have answers to all three, and I'm happy to put it in writing." |
| "Can you do it cheaper?" | "The quote reflects the scope you described. I can adjust the scope if the budget is tight — for example, we could do [reduced scope] for [lower price]. But I don't want to cut corners on quality." |
| "I'll call you back" | "Sounds good. I'll send the quote to your email so it's there when you're ready. If I don't hear back by [day], I'll check in once — just to make sure you have everything you need." |

### 17.4 Closing Patterns

**Soft close (best for most situations):**
> "Based on what you've told me, I'll put together a quote tonight and send it over. You'll get a link where you can review everything and accept it right from your phone — no paperwork. Sound good?"

**Urgency close (when timeline is tight):**
> "Since you need this done by [date], I'd recommend locking in the crew now — our schedule fills up fast in [season/month]. I can send the quote in the next hour."

**Recurring close (always attempt):**
> "A lot of our clients in similar buildings switch to a weekly or biweekly plan — it keeps the space consistently clean and saves about [X]% versus booking one-off. Want me to include a recurring option on the quote so you can compare?"

## 18) Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO) Program

Purpose: The website should get better at converting every month, not just stay static. This section defines the systematic testing and improvement process.

### 18.1 CRO Operating Principles

Prerequisite: CRO testing depends on F-25 (CTA Taxonomy + Funnel Instrumentation). Capture 1-2 weeks of baseline CTA data after F-25 before starting formal test cycles.

1. **One test at a time** per page or funnel stage. Never change two things simultaneously.
2. **Minimum 200 sessions** before drawing conclusions on any test.
3. **Measure conversion action**, not vanity metrics (clicks, time-on-page are supporting data, not decisions).
4. **Document every test** — what changed, what happened, what was decided.

### 18.2 Test Prioritization Framework

Score each potential test on three dimensions (1-5 each):

| Dimension | Question |
|---|---|
| **Impact** | If this wins, how much revenue does it affect? |
| **Confidence** | How sure are we this will improve conversion? |
| **Ease** | How fast can we implement and measure it? |

**ICE Score** = Impact + Confidence + Ease. Run highest-scored tests first.

### 18.3 High-Probability Test Queue (Starting Backlog)

| Test | CTA Being Tested | Measurement | Hypothesis | ICE |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hero CTA copy: "Get a Free Quote" vs "Get Your Custom Quote in 60 Seconds" | `hero_get_quote_primary` | CTA click-through rate | Specificity + speed claim increases click-through | 13 |
| Add "Response Within 1 Hour" to quote form header | `quote_form_submit` | Form submission rate | Trust signal at point of friction reduces abandonment | 12 |
| Add phone number to mobile sticky bar (click-to-call) | `mobile_sticky_call_now` | Click-to-call rate | Some buyers prefer calling; reducing friction captures them | 12 |
| Add 3 review snippets next to quote form | `quote_form_submit` | Submission completion rate | Social proof at decision point increases submissions | 11 |
| Service page CTA at mid-page (not just bottom) | `service_page_get_quote_mid` | Mid-page CTA conversion rate | Catches ready-to-act visitors before they scroll away | 11 |
| Exit-intent offer: "Get 10% off your first clean" vs current | `exit_intent_get_quote_overlay` | Overlay CTA conversion | Concrete incentive beats generic close-save prompt | 10 |
| Before/after slider on service pages (not just homepage) | `service_page_get_quote_bottom` | Page-to-form conversion | Visual proof near decision point improves trust | 10 |
| Confirmation page with referral ask | `confirmation_referral_cta` | Referral CTA click rate | Fresh leads are the warmest referral source | 9 |
